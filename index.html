Coulomb's Law

  • SUMMARY
  • LESSON
  • EXAMPLES
\[ \textbf{F} = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{qQ}{r^2}\mathbf{\hat{r}} \]
Describes the force of attraction between two charged particles
\( \textbf{F} \) The force experienced by test charge Q\( \mathrm{\big[\ N\ \big]} \)
\( q \)The magnitude of the source charge\( \mathrm{\big[\ C\ \big]} \)
\( Q \)The magnitude of the test charge\( \mathrm{\big[\ C\ \big]} \)
\( r \)The separation distance between the two charges\( \mathrm{\left[m\right]} \)
\( \mathbf{\hat{r}} \) A unit vector extending from the source charge, q, in the direction of the test charge, Q.\( \mathbf{\hat{r}} = \frac{\mathbf{r_1 - r_2}}{\| \mathbf{r_1 - r_2} \|} \)-
\( \epsilon_0 \)The permittivity of free space, an electromagnetic constant for a vacuum environment \( 8.85 * 10^{-12}\)\( \mathrm{\left[\frac{C^2}{N \cdot m^2}\right]} \)
